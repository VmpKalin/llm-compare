The error occurs in the step where we **divide both sides by (a - b)**.

Here's why it's invalid:

We started with the assumption that `a = b`.  This means that `a - b = 0`.  Division by zero is undefined and illegal in mathematics. Dividing both sides of an equation by a quantity that equals zero leads to a nonsensical result, as demonstrated here.

The step `(a+b)(a-b) = b(a-b)` implies dividing by `(a-b)`, but since `a=b`, we are effectively dividing by 0.  This invalidates the entire subsequent argument and allows us to "prove" false statements like 2=1.

In short, you can't divide by zero! It breaks the rules of algebra.
