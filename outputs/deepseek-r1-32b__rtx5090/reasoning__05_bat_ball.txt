Let's solve the problem step by step.

**Given:**
- Total cost of a bat and a ball: **1.10 euros**
- The bat costs **1.00 euro more** than the ball.

**Let’s define:**
- Let \( b \) be the cost of the **ball** in euros.
- Then, the cost of the **bat** will be \( b + 1.00 \) euros.

**Set up the equation based on the total cost:**
\[
b \quad (\text{ball}) + (b + 1.00) \quad (\text{bat}) = 1.10
\]

**Simplify the equation:**
\[
2b + 1.00 = 1.10
\]

**Subtract 1.00 from both sides to solve for \( b \):**
\[
2b = 0.10
\]
\[
b = \frac{0.10}{2} = 0.05
\]

**Find the cost of the bat:**
\[
\text{Bat} = b + 1.00 = 0.05 + 1.00 = 1.05
\]

**Final Answer:**
The ball costs \(\boxed{0.05}\) euros.
