Let's solve the problem step by step.

**Given:**
- Total cost of a bat and a ball: **1.10 euros**
- The bat costs **1.00 euro more** than the ball.

**Let’s define:**
- Let \( x \) be the cost of the **ball** in euros.
- Then, the cost of the **bat** would be \( x + 1.00 \) euros.

**Set up the equation based on the total cost:**
\[
\text{Cost of ball} + \text{Cost of bat} = 1.10
\]
\[
x + (x + 1.00) = 1.10
\]

**Simplify and solve for \( x \):**
\[
2x + 1.00 = 1.10
\]
\[
2x = 1.10 - 1.00
\]
\[
2x = 0.10
\]
\[
x = \frac{0.10}{2}
\]
\[
x = 0.05
\]

**Conclusion:**
The ball costs **€0.05**.

\[
\boxed{0.05\,\text{euros}}
\]
