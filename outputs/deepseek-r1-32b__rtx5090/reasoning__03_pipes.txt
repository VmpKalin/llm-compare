To determine how long it will take to fill the tank when all three pipes (A, B, and C) are open, we can follow these steps:

### **Step 1: Determine the Filling Rates**

- **Pipe A** fills the tank in **6 hours**, so its rate is:
  
  \[
  \text{Rate of Pipe A} = \frac{1}{6} \text{ tank per hour}
  \]

- **Pipe B** fills the tank in **4 hours**, so its rate is:
  
  \[
  \text{Rate of Pipe B} = \frac{1}{4} \text{ tank per hour}
  \]

- **Pipe C** empties the tank in **12 hours**, so its rate is:
  
  \[
  \text{Rate of Pipe C} = -\frac{1}{12} \text{ tank per hour}
  \]
  
  *(Note: The negative sign indicates that Pipe C is emptying the tank.)*

### **Step 2: Combine the Rates**

When all three pipes are open, their rates add up:

\[
\text{Combined Rate} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{12}
\]

To combine these fractions, find a common denominator. The least common multiple of 6, 4, and 12 is **12**:

\[
\text{Combined Rate} = \frac{2}{12} + \frac{3}{12} - \frac{1}{12} = \frac{4}{12} = \frac{1}{3}
\]

So, the combined rate is \( \frac{1}{3} \) of the tank per hour.

### **Step 3: Calculate Time to Fill the Tank**

If the pipes together fill \( \frac{1}{3} \) of the tank in one hour, then the time (\( t \)) required to fill the entire tank is:

\[
t = \frac{1}{\text{Combined Rate}} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{3}} = 3 \text{ hours}
\]

### **Final Answer**

The tank will be full in:

\[
\boxed{3\,\text{hours}}
