To determine how long it will take to fill the tank with all three pipes open, let's analyze their rates:

1. **Pipe A** fills the tank in **6 hours**, so its rate is:
   \[
   \text{Rate of Pipe A} = \frac{1}{6} \text{ tanks per hour}
   \]

2. **Pipe B** fills the tank in **4 hours**, so its rate is:
   \[
   \text{Rate of Pipe B} = \frac{1}{4} \text{ tanks per hour}
   \]

3. **Pipe C** empties the tank in **12 hours**, so its rate is:
   \[
   \text{Rate of Pipe C} = -\frac{1}{12} \text{ tanks per hour}
   \]
   *(Note: The negative sign indicates emptying)*

4. **Combined Rate** when all three pipes are open:
   \[
   \text{Total Rate} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{12}
   \]

5. **Simplify the Combined Rate**:
   \[
   \text{Total Rate} = \frac{2}{12} + \frac{3}{12} - \frac{1}{12} = \frac{4}{12} = \frac{1}{3} \text{ tanks per hour}
   \]

6. **Time to Fill the Tank**:
   Since the combined rate is \( \frac{1}{3} \) tanks per hour, the time (\( T \)) required to fill 1 tank is:
   \[
   T = \frac{1}{\text{Total Rate}} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{3}} = 3 \text{ hours}
   \]

**Final Answer:**
\[
\boxed{3 \text{ hours}}
