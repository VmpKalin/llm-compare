To solve this problem, we need to write a PostgreSQL query that returns, for each country, the number of customers who placed at least one order in 2025 and their total revenue in euros. The result should be ordered by revenue in descending order.

### Approach
1. **Join Tables**: We will join the `customers` table with the `orders` table using the `customer_id` to link each customer with their orders.
2. **Filter Orders by Year**: We filter the orders to include only those placed in the year 2025 using the `created_at` column.
3. **Group by Country**: After filtering, we group the results by the country of the customers.
4. **Count Customers**: For each country, count the distinct customers who have placed at least one order in 2025 to avoid counting the same customer multiple times if they placed multiple orders.
5. **Calculate Total Revenue**: Convert the total revenue from cents to euros by summing up `total_cents` and dividing by 100.
6. **Order Results**: Finally, we order the results by total revenue in descending order.

### Solution Code
```sql
SELECT c.country, COUNT(DISTINCT c.id) AS customer_count, (SUM(o.total_cents) / 100) AS total_revenue_euros
FROM customers c
JOIN orders o ON c.id = o.customer_id
WHERE EXTRACT(year FROM o.created_at) = 2025
GROUP BY c.country
ORDER BY total_revenue_euros DESC;
```

### Explanation
- **Joining Tables**: The `JOIN` operation combines the `customers` and `orders` tables based on the `customer_id`, allowing us to access both customer details and their orders.
- **Filtering by Year**: Using `EXTRACT(year FROM o.created_at) = 2025`, we ensure only orders placed in 2025 are considered.
- **Grouping by Country**: The `GROUP BY` clause groups the results by each country, allowing us to aggregate data per country.
- **Counting Customers**: `COUNT(DISTINCT c.id)` ensures each customer is counted once even if they have multiple orders.
- **Calculating Revenue**: Summing `total_cents` and dividing by 100 converts the total revenue from cents to euros.
- **Ordering Results**: The results are ordered by total revenue in descending order using `ORDER BY total_revenue_euros DESC`.

This approach efficiently combines data from both tables, applies necessary filters, and aggregates the required metrics while ensuring clarity and correctness in the result set.
