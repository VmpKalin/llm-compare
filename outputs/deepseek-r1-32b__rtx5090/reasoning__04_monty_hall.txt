In the Monty Hall problem, you initially have a 1/3 chance of picking the car and a 2/3 chance of picking a goat. When the host reveals a goat behind another door, switching allows you to capitalize on the higher probability (2/3) that your initial choice was incorrect. Therefore, switching increases your chances of winning from 1/3 to 2/3.

**Answer:**

Yes, you should switch. The probability of winning if you switch is **2/3**, while staying with your original choice gives a **1/3** chance of winning. This is because the host's action provides information that increases the likelihood the car is behind the other unopened door.
