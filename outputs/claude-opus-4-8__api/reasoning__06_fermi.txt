# Estimating Piano Tuners in Berlin

Let me work through this step by step using a Fermi estimation approach.

## Step 1: Population of Berlin
Berlin is Germany's largest city. I'll estimate its population at roughly **3.5 million people**.

## Step 2: Number of Pianos
I need to estimate how many pianos exist in Berlin.

- **Households:** Assume ~2 people per household → about **1.75 million households**.
- **Pianos in homes:** Germany has a fairly strong musical culture, but pianos are still a minority item. I'll estimate about **1 in 30 households** owns a piano → ~58,000 home pianos.
- **Institutional pianos:** Schools, churches, music academies, concert halls, bars, etc. Let me add roughly **20%** more to account for these → ~12,000 institutional pianos.

**Total pianos ≈ 70,000**

## Step 3: Tuning Frequency
- Pianos are ideally tuned about once or twice per year.
- But many home pianos get tuned less often (neglected), while institutional/professional pianos get tuned more.
- I'll use an average of **1 tuning per piano per year**.

**Total tunings per year ≈ 70,000**

## Step 4: Tuner Workload
How many tunings can one full-time tuner perform per year?

- A tuning takes about **1.5–2 hours**, plus travel time between clients.
- Realistically a tuner might do **~3 tunings per working day**.
- Working ~**220 days per year** (accounting for weekends, holidays, sick days, admin).

**Tunings per tuner per year ≈ 3 × 220 = 660**, round to **~650**.

## Step 5: Final Calculation

$$
\text{Tuners} = \frac{70,000 \text{ tunings/year}}{650 \text{ tunings/tuner/year}} \approx 108
$$

## Final Estimate

**Roughly 100 piano tuners in Berlin** (order of magnitude: ~50–150).

---

### Key uncertainties
- **Piano ownership rate** is the biggest wildcard — if it's 1 in 20 rather than 1 in 30, the number jumps by 50%.
- **Tuning frequency** could easily be lower in practice (many pianos go untuned for years), pulling the estimate down.
- Some tuners work part-time or combine tuning with repair/sales work, which would increase the headcount but reduce full-time-equivalents.

Given these offsetting factors, **~100 tuners** feels like a reasonable central estimate.
