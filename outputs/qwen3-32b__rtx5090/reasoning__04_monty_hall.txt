In the Monty Hall problem, you should switch doors. If you stay with your initial choice (door 1), you have a **1/3 chance of winning** the car, as your initial selection had a 1/3 probability of being correct. However, if you switch to door 2, your probability of winning increases to **2/3**. This is because the host’s action of revealing a goat (door 3) provides additional information: if the car was initially behind door 2 (which occurs 2/3 of the time), the host is forced to open the other goat door, making switching the optimal strategy. Thus, switching leverages the higher likelihood that your initial choice was wrong.
