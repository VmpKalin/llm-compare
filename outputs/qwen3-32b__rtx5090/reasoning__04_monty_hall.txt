In the Monty Hall problem, you should switch doors. If you stay with your initial choice (door 1), you have a **1/3** chance of winning the car, as your initial selection had a 1/3 probability of being correct. If you switch to door 2, your probability of winning increases to **2/3**. This is because the host’s action of revealing a goat (door 3) provides additional information: if the car was initially behind door 2 or 3 (which happens 2/3 of the time), the host effectively eliminates the wrong door, leaving the car behind the unchosen door. Switching capitalizes on the higher likelihood that your initial guess was wrong.
