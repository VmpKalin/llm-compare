The problem states that a bat and a ball together cost **1.10 euros**, and the bat costs **1.00 euro more than the ball**. We are asked to determine the cost of the ball.

---

### Step 1: Define Variables

Let the cost of the **ball** be $ x $ euros.  
Then, the cost of the **bat** is $ x + 1 $ euros, since it is 1 euro more than the ball.

---

### Step 2: Set Up the Equation

The total cost of the bat and the ball is given as 1.10 euros. So:

$$
x + (x + 1) = 1.10
$$

Simplify the left-hand side:

$$
2x + 1 = 1.10
$$

---

### Step 3: Solve for $ x $

Subtract 1 from both sides:

$$
2x = 0.10
$$

Divide both sides by 2:

$$
x = 0.05
$$

---

### Step 4: Interpret the Result

The **ball costs 0.05 euros**, or **5 cents**.  
Then the **bat costs** $ 0.05 + 1.00 = 1.05 $ euros.  
Adding the two together:  
$$
0.05 + 1.05 = 1.10
$$

This satisfies the condition that the bat costs 1 euro more than the ball and that the total cost is 1.10 euros.
