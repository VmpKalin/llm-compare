To determine how long it takes to fill the tank when all three pipes are open, we analyze the **rates** at which each pipe contributes to the filling or emptying of the tank.

---

### Step 1: Determine Individual Rates

- **Pipe A** fills the tank in 6 hours → rate = $ \frac{1}{6} $ tanks/hour  
- **Pipe B** fills the tank in 4 hours → rate = $ \frac{1}{4} $ tanks/hour  
- **Pipe C** empties the tank in 12 hours → rate = $ -\frac{1}{12} $ tanks/hour (negative because it's emptying)

---

### Step 2: Combine the Rates

We sum the rates to find the **net rate** at which the tank is being filled:

$$
\text{Net rate} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{12}
$$

To add these, we find a common denominator. The least common denominator of 6, 4, and 12 is 12:

$$
\frac{1}{6} = \frac{2}{12}, \quad \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{12}, \quad \frac{1}{12} = \frac{1}{12}
$$

$$
\text{Net rate} = \frac{2}{12} + \frac{3}{12} - \frac{1}{12} = \frac{4}{12} = \frac{1}{3} \text{ tanks/hour}
$$

---

### Step 3: Calculate Time to Fill the Tank

If the net rate is $ \frac{1}{3} $ tanks per hour, then the time $ T $ to fill **1 full tank** is:

$$
T = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{3}} = 3 \text{ hours}
$$

---

### Final Answer

- **As a fraction of hours:** $ \boxed{3} $
- **In hours and minutes:** 3 hours and 0 minutes
