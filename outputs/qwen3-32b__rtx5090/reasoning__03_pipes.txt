To determine how long it takes to fill the tank when all three pipes are open, we analyze the **rates** at which each pipe contributes to or removes from the tank.

---

### Step 1: Determine the individual rates

- **Pipe A** fills the tank in 6 hours → rate = $ \frac{1}{6} $ tank/hour  
- **Pipe B** fills the tank in 4 hours → rate = $ \frac{1}{4} $ tank/hour  
- **Pipe C** empties the tank in 12 hours → rate = $ -\frac{1}{12} $ tank/hour  

---

### Step 2: Combine the rates

When all three pipes are open, the **net rate** at which the tank is being filled is the sum of their individual rates:

$$
\text{Net rate} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{12}
$$

To add these, we find a common denominator (LCM of 6, 4, and 12 is 12):

$$
\frac{1}{6} = \frac{2}{12}, \quad \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{12}, \quad \frac{1}{12} = \frac{1}{12}
$$

$$
\text{Net rate} = \frac{2}{12} + \frac{3}{12} - \frac{1}{12} = \frac{4}{12} = \frac{1}{3} \text{ tank/hour}
$$

---

### Step 3: Calculate the time to fill the tank

If the tank is being filled at a rate of $ \frac{1}{3} $ tank per hour, then the time $ t $ to fill **1 full tank** is:

$$
t = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{3}} = 3 \text{ hours}
$$

---

### Final Answer

- **As a fraction of hours**: $ \boxed{3} $
- **In hours and minutes**: 3 hours and 0 minutes
