# Tank Filling Problem

## Step 1: Find the rate for each pipe

**Pipe A:** Fills tank in 6 hours
- Rate = 1/6 tank per hour

**Pipe B:** Fills tank in 4 hours
- Rate = 1/4 tank per hour

**Pipe C:** Empties tank in 12 hours
- Rate = -1/12 tank per hour (negative because it empties)

## Step 2: Find the combined rate

Combined rate = 1/6 + 1/4 - 1/12

Finding common denominator (LCD = 12):
$$\frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{12} = \frac{2}{12} + \frac{3}{12} - \frac{1}{12} = \frac{4}{12} = \frac{1}{3}$$

## Step 3: Calculate time to fill

If the combined rate is 1/3 tank per hour, then:

$$\text{Time} = \frac{1 \text{ tank}}{1/3 \text{ tank per hour}} = 3 \text{ hours}$$

## Final Answer

**As a fraction:** **3 hours** (or 3/1 hours)

**In hours and minutes:** **3 hours 0 minutes**
