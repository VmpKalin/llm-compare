# Monty Hall Problem Analysis

**You should switch.**

- **If you stay with door 1:** Probability of winning = **1/3**
- **If you switch to door 2:** Probability of winning = **2/3**

**Why:** When you initially picked door 1, there was only a 1/3 chance it had the car. The host's action of revealing a goat behind door 3 doesn't change your door's probability—it remains 1/3. This means the car is behind door 2 with probability 2/3 (the remaining probability). Switching concentrates the 2/3 probability onto your new choice.
