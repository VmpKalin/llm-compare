# Estimating Piano Tuners in Berlin

Let me work through this systematically.

## Step 1: Berlin's Population
Berlin has roughly **3.5-4 million people**. I'll use **3.7 million**.

## Step 2: How Many Pianos in Berlin?

Pianos are luxury/specialized instruments. I need to estimate what fraction of households have one.

- **Household size**: ~2.2 people per household = ~1.7 million households
- **Piano ownership rate**: This is the tricky part. Pianos are expensive and require space. In a modern city:
  - Maybe 2-3% of households have pianos? (includes upright and grand pianos)
  - This seems reasonable for a wealthy European city with cultural institutions
  - 1.7M × 0.025 = **~42,500 household pianos**

- **Institutional pianos** (concert halls, music schools, churches, studios):
  - Berlin has a strong music culture (orchestras, conservatories, etc.)
  - Maybe 500-1000 institutional pianos?
  - I'll add **~1,000 institutional pianos**

**Total pianos: ~43,500**

## Step 3: Tuning Frequency

How often does a piano need tuning?

- **Household pianos** (casual use): 1-2 times per year, let's say **1.5 times/year**
- **Institutional pianos** (heavy use): 4-12 times per year, let's say **6 times/year**

**Total tunings per year:**
- Household: 42,500 × 1.5 = 63,750
- Institutional: 1,000 × 6 = 6,000
- **Total: ~70,000 tunings/year**

## Step 4: Piano Tuner Workload

How many pianos can one tuner service per year?

- **Time per tuning**: 1-2 hours, plus travel time. Let's say **2.5 hours total** per piano
- **Working hours per year**: 
  - ~250 working days/year
  - ~6-7 hours of billable work per day (accounting for admin, travel gaps)
  - ~1,500 billable hours/year

**Pianos per tuner per year:**
- 1,500 hours ÷ 2.5 hours per piano = **~600 pianos/year**

## Step 5: Calculate Number of Tuners

70,000 tunings ÷ 600 tunings per tuner = **~117 piano tuners**

## Final Estimate

**Roughly 100-120 piano tuners in Berlin**

This feels reasonable because:
- It's a major cultural city with above-average piano ownership
- But pianos are still niche instruments
- The number scales with population and wealth
- For comparison, a city of 3.7M might have ~3,700 plumbers; piano tuners should be much rarer (maybe 1-2% as common)
