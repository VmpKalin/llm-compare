# Estimating Piano Tuners in Berlin

## Step 1: Population of Berlin

Berlin is Germany's capital and largest city. I'll estimate roughly **3.5 million people**.

---

## Step 2: Number of Pianos in Berlin

Not every household has a piano — it's a relatively expensive, space-consuming instrument. I'll think about this in layers:

- Average household size in a dense European city: ~**2 people per household**
- That gives roughly **1.75 million households**
- Piano ownership is more common in Germany than many countries (strong musical culture), but still a minority hobby
- I'd estimate maybe **1 in 20 households** owns a piano → that's a 5% ownership rate
- 1,750,000 × 0.05 = **~87,500 household pianos**

But pianos also exist in institutions: schools, churches, hotels, concert halls, music schools, bars, rehearsal studios. Berlin has a particularly rich cultural scene. I'll add maybe **30% more** for institutional pianos.

- 87,500 × 1.3 ≈ **~115,000 pianos total**

---

## Step 3: How Often Are Pianos Tuned?

- A well-maintained piano should be tuned **2 times per year**
- Many household pianos are neglected — maybe tuned once every 2 years on average
- Institutional pianos (concert halls, music schools) are tuned far more frequently — some weekly
- Blending these: I'll estimate an **average of 1 tuning per piano per year** across the whole stock

- Total tunings per year: **115,000 × 1 = ~115,000 tunings/year**

---

## Step 4: How Much Work Can One Tuner Do?

- A tuning takes roughly **1.5 hours** including travel time in a city
- A tuner works ~**5 days/week**, ~**48 weeks/year** (allowing for holidays, admin)
- Working hours per day: ~**6 productive hours** (travel, setup, tuning)
- Tunings per day: 6 ÷ 1.5 = **~4 tunings/day**
- Tunings per year per tuner: 4 × 5 × 48 = **~960 tunings/year**

Call it roughly **1,000 tunings per tuner per year**.

---

## Step 5: Final Calculation

$$\text{Tuners} = \frac{\text{Total tunings/year}}{\text{Tunings per tuner/year}} = \frac{115{,}000}{1{,}000} \approx \mathbf{115}$$

---

## Result: Roughly **100–150 piano tuners** in Berlin

### Sanity Check
- That's about 1 tuner per 30,000 people — plausible for a skilled niche trade
- Berlin likely has *more* than average due to its arts/music culture, which my piano count already partially captures
- The number feels right: not so few that they'd be overwhelmed, not so many that it's implausible as a livelihood

**Final answer: ~120 piano tuners**
