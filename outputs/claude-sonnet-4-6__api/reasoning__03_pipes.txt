# Tank Filling Problem

## Setting Up the Rates

Find each pipe's rate as a **fraction of the tank per hour**:

| Pipe | Action | Rate (tank/hour) |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| A | Fills | +1/6 |
| B | Fills | +1/4 |
| C | Empties | −1/12 |

---

## Combined Rate

$$\text{Net Rate} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{12}$$

Find a common denominator (LCD = 12):

$$= \frac{2}{12} + \frac{3}{12} - \frac{1}{12} = \frac{4}{12} = \frac{1}{3} \text{ tank/hour}$$

---

## Time to Fill the Tank

$$\text{Time} = \frac{1 \text{ tank}}{\frac{1}{3} \text{ tank/hour}} = \boxed{3 \text{ hours}}$$

---

## Final Answer

> **3 hours exactly** (3 hours, 0 minutes)

The draining effect of pipe C is exactly offset by the extra capacity of pipes A and B together, resulting in a clean fill time of **3 hours**.
