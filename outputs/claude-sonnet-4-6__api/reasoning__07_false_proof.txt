## The Flaw: Division by Zero

The error occurs at the step:

> **"Divide both sides by (a − b)"**

---

### Why It's Invalid

The proof begins by setting **a = b**, which means:

$$a - b = 0$$

Dividing both sides by $(a - b)$ is therefore **dividing by zero**, which is undefined and not a valid algebraic operation.

---

### What's Actually Happening

Up to the factoring step, everything is legitimate:

$$(a+b)(a-b) = b(a-b)$$

This is a **true statement** — but it's actually just $0 = 0$, since both sides contain the factor $(a - b) = 0$.

When you "cancel" $(a-b)$ from both sides, you are not simplifying — you are performing:

$$\frac{(a+b)\cdot 0}{0} = \frac{b \cdot 0}{0}$$

which is **undefined**. Any conclusion drawn after this step is meaningless.

---

### Key Takeaway

Division by zero can be disguised — here it's hidden inside an algebraic expression $(a-b)$ that *looks* nonzero but isn't. This is a classic technique in false proofs: **smuggle in a zero, divide by it, derive nonsense**.
