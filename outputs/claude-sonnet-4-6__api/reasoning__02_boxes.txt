## Draw from the "Apples & Oranges" box.

**Reasoning:**

Since **every label is wrong**, the "Apples & Oranges" box cannot contain a mix — it must contain *either* apples only *or* oranges only.

- Say you draw an **apple**. Then that box is **Apples only**.
- Now the remaining boxes ("Apples" and "Oranges") are both mislabeled.
- The box labeled "Apples" can't be apples (wrong label) and can't be apples-only (taken) → it must be **Oranges & Apples (the mix)**.
- The box labeled "Oranges" must then be **Oranges only**.

The same logic applies symmetrically if you draw an orange.

**Why not the other boxes?** Drawing from "Apples" or "Oranges" could yield a fruit consistent with two possible arrangements, leaving ambiguity. The mislabeled "Apples & Oranges" box is the only one guaranteed to be a *pure* single-fruit box, giving you a definitive starting point to resolve all three.
