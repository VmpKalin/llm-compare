## Setting Up the Problem

Let me define variables:
- Let **b** = cost of the ball (in euros)
- Let **B** = cost of the bat (in euros)

## The Two Equations

**Equation 1** (total cost):
$$B + b = 1.10$$

**Equation 2** (the bat costs €1.00 more than the ball):
$$B = b + 1.00$$

## Solving

Substitute Equation 2 into Equation 1:

$$(b + 1.00) + b = 1.10$$

$$2b + 1.00 = 1.10$$

$$2b = 0.10$$

$$b = 0.05$$

## Answer

**The ball costs €0.05 (5 cents).**

The bat therefore costs €1.05, and indeed:
- €1.05 + €0.05 = **€1.10** ✓
- €1.05 − €0.05 = **€1.00** ✓

---

> 💡 The intuitive (but wrong) answer is 10 cents. If the ball cost €0.10, the bat would cost €1.10, totalling €1.20 — not €1.10. The correct answer, **5 cents**, is a classic example of how our intuitive thinking can lead us astray.
