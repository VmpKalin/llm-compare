Pipe B fills the tank in 4 hours, so its rate is:
Rate of B = 1/4 tanks per hour

Pipe C empties the tank in 12 hours, so its rate is:
Rate of C = -1/12 tanks per hour (negative because it empties)

When all three pipes are open simultaneously, the net rate of filling is:
Net rate = Rate of A + Rate of B + Rate of C
Net rate = 1/6 + 1/4 + (-1/12)
Net rate = 1/6 + 1/4 - 1/12

To add these fractions, I need a common denominator. The LCD of 6, 4, and 12 is 12.

Converting each fraction:
- 1/6 = 2/12
- 1/4 = 3/12  
- 1/12 = 1/12

Net rate = 2/12 + 3/12 - 1/12 = (2 + 3 - 1)/12 = 4/12 = 1/3 tanks per hour

If the net rate is 1/3 tanks per hour, then the time to fill 1 complete tank is:
Time = 1 ÷ (1/3) = 1 × 3/1 = 3 hours
